Department of Epidemiology and Preventive Medicine

Profile
The Department of Veterinary Public Health and Epidemiology was carved out of the pre-existing Department of Veterinary Microbiology and Public Health in 1995. The department is closely engaged in teaching, research and extension activities of the University and also associated with the organization and/ or participation in seminars, workshops and training programmes from time to time.

Objectives and Mandate

  1. To impart the professional Veterinary Public Health and Epidemiology knowledge and skills in undergraduate students.
  2. To develop expertise to undertake epidemiological investigations on zoonotic diseases of major public health importance.
  3. To increase professional and public awareness on Veterinary Public Health and Food Safety issues.
  4. To undertake research activities on Zoonotic and Food Borne Diseases.

B. RESEARCH:
 Department is involved in conducting research work of field application in different areas of Veterinary Public Health and Epidemiology.
Research Areas include

  1. Food Safety
  2. Milk Hygiene
  3. Meat, egg, fish hygiene
  4. Zoonoses
  5. Zoonotic diseases       
  6. Food borne infection and intoxication
  7. Epidemiological investigation of diseases

Environmental Hygiene

  1. Water quality
  2. Air quality
  3. Waste disposal

C. EXTENSION ACTIVITIES:

The staff of the department is actively involved in the extension activities for the benefits of farmers. Some of the routine extension activities undertaken are;

  1. Providing information on Zoonotic diseases to farmer, Self Help Groups etc.,
  2. Participation in Pashumelas or Livestock shows
  3. Radio talks/ TV shows
  4. Training programmes to Field Veterinarians/farmers/Self Help Groups
  5. Consultancy Service
  6. Preparation of Leaflets/Brochure on Veterinary Public Health and Epidemiology
